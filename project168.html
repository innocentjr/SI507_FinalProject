The Lee Pesky Learning Center

Last updated Tuesday, October 17, 2017

Quick Facts

Current Phase
  • In development
Issue Area
  • Early Childhood
Service Period
  • Less than 3

The Lee Pesky Learning Center (LPLC) is a Boise, ID based nonprofit providing psycho-educational evaluations and academic and counseling interventions to students at-risk for or with high-incidence disabilities. In an effort to ensure more Idaho public school children are reading at grade level, this Pay for Success contract targets children in Idaho public elementary schools at risk for not reading at benchmark reading levels in grades K-3 and at risk for tracking into special education for ongoing literacy support grade 3. Through this Pay for Success project, the Pesky Center’s evidence-based early literacy intervention provides professional development to early care providers and K-3 teachers. The Pesky Center’s research shows that teachers trained using the Pesky Center’s intervention are more likely to have children reading at grade level than those who do not receive the training.

The Lee Pesky Learning Center

LPLC was founded by a loving, compassionate family whose son struggled with a learning disability his entire life. Since opening our doors in 1997, we’ve helped thousands of people with learning disabilities learn how to read, write, and do math through our individual learning services.

Idaho State Department of Education

The Idaho State Department of Education (SDE) is a government agency supporting schools and students. We are responsible for implementing policies, distributing funds, administering statewide assessments, licensing educators, and providing accountability data.
